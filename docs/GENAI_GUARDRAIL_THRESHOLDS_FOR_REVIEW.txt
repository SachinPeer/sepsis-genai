================================================================================
         GENAI SEPSIS GUARDRAIL - CLINICAL THRESHOLDS FOR SME REVIEW
================================================================================

Document Version: 3.0
Last Updated: February 2026
Purpose: Safety layer to override AI predictions when critical thresholds breached

================================================================================
                              HOW IT WORKS
================================================================================

These are HARD LIMITS. If ANY threshold is breached AND AI risk score < 80,
the system will OVERRIDE to CRITICAL (risk score = 95).

The guardrail acts as a safety net - ensuring no critical patient is missed
even if the AI model underestimates the risk.

================================================================================
                         CRITICAL THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HEMODYNAMIC
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Parameter                | Threshold | Condition | Unit    | Clinical Rationale
-------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------|--------------------
Systolic BP (SBP)        | 90        | ≤         | mmHg    | Sepsis-3 septic shock criterion

  SME Notes: Suggestive of sepsis if hypotension persists AFTER fluid 
  resuscitation. Slightly low SBP often indicates dehydration - if normalizes
  after fluids, probably not septic shock. TRENDING IS IMPORTANT.
  
  Context Check: Assess for bleeding, dehydration, PE, anaphylaxis, or 
  cardiogenic shock.

-------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------|--------------------
Mean Arterial Pressure   | 65        | <         | mmHg    | Vasopressor threshold (SSC)

  SME Notes: If lactate > 2 plus MAP < 65, suggests likely septic shock.
  If history of chronic HTN, MAP < 75 suggests septic shock.
  
  Context Check: Check lactate level and history of chronic hypertension.

-------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------|--------------------
MAP (HTN Patient)        | 75        | <         | mmHg    | Adjusted for chronic HTN

  Applies When: Patient has documented history of chronic hypertension.
  Clinical Rationale: Chronic HTN patients have higher baseline MAP.

-------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------|--------------------
Diastolic BP (DBP)       | 40        | <         | mmHg    | Late-stage shock indicator

================================================================================
                      EARLY DETECTION PATTERNS
================================================================================

EARLIEST RELIABLE LAB COMBINATION FOR BACTERIAL INFECTION:
  Requires ALL of:
    • Lymphocytes < 1.0 K/µL
    • Neutrophils > 80%
    • Bands ≥ 10%
  
  Action: High suspicion for serious bacterial infection - escalate priority


================================================================================
                     HISTORY/CONTEXT CHECKS
================================================================================

The system will check patient history and note relevant conditions in alerts:

RENAL HISTORY
  Check for: Renal disease, CKD, ESRF, renal insufficiency, dialysis
  Impact: Elevated creatinine may be baseline if history exists

HEPATIC HISTORY
  Check for: Liver disease, hepatitis, cirrhosis
  Impact: Elevated bilirubin/AST/ALT may be baseline if history exists

HEMATOLOGIC HISTORY
  Check for: Thrombocytopenia, ITP, leukemia, chemotherapy
  Impact: Low platelets or abnormal WBC may be baseline

CARDIOVASCULAR HISTORY
  Check for: Chronic hypertension (HTN)
  Impact: Use MAP < 75 (not < 65) for shock suspicion

DIABETES HISTORY
  Check for: Diabetes, DM, insulin dependent
  Impact: Compare glucose to baseline, note if ≥ 180

NEUROLOGIC HISTORY (for AMS assessment)
  Check for: Alzheimer's, dementia, delirium, alcoholism (ETOH),
             substance abuse, cognitive dysfunction
  Impact: Altered mental status must be NEW ONSET to be significant

SEIZURE HISTORY
  Check for: Seizure disorder, epilepsy, recent seizures
  Impact: Can elevate lactate independent of sepsis

MEDICATIONS
  Corticosteroids: Can elevate glucose
  Antipyretics: Can mask fever (within 4 hours)
  Anticoagulants: INR may be elevated due to medication, not DIC


================================================================================
                    DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
================================================================================

FOR HYPOTENSION - Also Assess:
  • Bleeding / Hemorrhage
  • Dehydration / Volume depletion
  • Pulmonary embolism (PE)
  • Anaphylaxis
  • Cardiogenic shock
  • Medication effect (antihypertensives, sedatives)

  Action: If any present, include in alert as differential diagnosis.


================================================================================
                    TRENDING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

LACTATE
  Action: Lactate > 2.0 requires REFLEX LACTATE after fluid resuscitation
  Timing: Repeat 2-4 hours after initial result and/or after fluid bolus
  Interpretation: Decrease with fluids = less concerning
                  Persists or rises = ongoing hypoperfusion

BLOOD PRESSURE
  Action: SBP < 90 requires assessment of fluid responsiveness
  Interpretation: Normalizes after fluids = likely dehydration
                  Persists = likely septic shock

URINE OUTPUT
  Action: Track hourly for at least 2-6 hours
  Interpretation: < 0.5 mL/kg/hr for 2+ hours = concerning
                  < 0.3 mL/kg/hr for 6+ hours = critical


================================================================================
                       OVERRIDE LOGIC
================================================================================

STANDARD OVERRIDE:
  IF (Any Critical Threshold Breached) AND (AI Risk Score < 80)
  THEN → Force Risk Score to 95, Priority to "Critical"

SEPTIC SHOCK DETECTION:
  IF (SBP ≤ 90 OR MAP < 65) AND (Lactate ≥ 2.0)
  THEN → Immediate escalation to Septic Shock classification

DIC DETECTION:
  IF 3 or more of:
    • Platelets < 50 K/µL
    • INR ≥ 2.5
    • Fibrinogen < 100 mg/dL
    • D-Dimer ≥ 10 µg/mL
  THEN → Flag as DIC, recommend hematology consult


================================================================================
               SILENT SEPSIS DETECTION (Nursing Notes)
================================================================================

The system searches nursing notes for concerning phrases that may indicate
deterioration even when vitals appear stable.

PERFUSION CONCERNS:
  • mottled skin / mottling
  • skin cool to touch / cool extremities
  • delayed capillary refill / cap refill > 3 seconds
  • poor perfusion / cyanosis / pallor

MENTAL STATUS (must be NEW ONSET):
  • altered mental status / AMS
  • confused / confusion
  • lethargic / lethargy / drowsy
  • obtunded / somnolent
  • disoriented / agitated / combative
  
  Exclusions: Check for existing Alzheimer's, dementia, alcoholism,
              substance abuse, cognitive dysfunction

FLUID RESPONSE:
  • fluid bolus with minimal response
  • unresponsive to fluids / fluid refractory
  • persistent hypotension after fluids

VASOPRESSOR USE:
  • vasopressors / requiring pressors / pressor support
  • levophed / norepinephrine / vasopressin
  • dopamine drip / epinephrine drip

URINE OUTPUT:
  • oliguria / decreased urine output / low urine output
  • minimal urine / no urine output / anuria

RESPIRATORY DISTRESS:
  • increased work of breathing / WOB
  • labored breathing / respiratory distress
  • dyspnea / air hunger
  • retractions (intercostal, supraclavicular, substernal)
  • accessory muscle use / nasal flaring
  • tracheal tug / shoulder lifting with respirations
  • head bobbing / speaking in 2-3 word sentences
  • tripoding / orthopnea / unable to lie flat

================================================================================
                        KEY PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

  1. Trending is often more important than single values
  2. Context matters - always check patient history
  3. NEW ONSET changes are more significant than chronic abnormalities
  4. Consider differential diagnoses before concluding sepsis
  5. Document rationale for overrides in alert scripts
